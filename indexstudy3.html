Case Study #3 Redesign of Non-Profit web site. Literacy Action Center web site.

The Problem: The existing LAC web site is a wed site that does not make the user feel as though they could trust this non-profit. Imagery was lacking and the navigation is inconsistent and confusing.

User Persona

The User Persona was was created with 9 interviews each team member doing 3 from the interviews we gathered that the potental best canadate to volunteer to help out this non-profit would be 1 some one with extra time. 2 a person that wants to help others. 3 a person that has a teaching background.

Competitor Analysis

This form does not show much I placed it here to help visualize the process. There a thousand different versions of this form. What I learned by using this form was the importance of research and doing your homework on who you are competing with. Not knowing who you are up against can come back and bit you.

Lo-fi Prototype

This low-Fidality prototype was never used for the final prototype. This prototype was used to get the conversation started. The team went a diffrent direction after user testing. The prototype was created based on conversations with the team and user testing that we did as a team. In the end we went another way. We used a lo-fi protype to get the answers we needed "Fail offten fail quickly" In the end we had a superior product.

The Solution: we as a team decided that we needed to make information about the people that founded it and manage the non-profit front and center. We added a page that talked about the history and founders. We also added a page that showcased all there good workes in videos nd testimonals. We made the donation process easy as a one button submition. We also streamlined the navigation from 20 choices going both vertical and horizontal to 5.

What I Learned: 1. Follow the Map: The site map gave the team an idea about the web sites structure. With the site map we were able to locate and identify the required pages.

What I Learned: 2. What The Other Guy Is Doing: The Competitor Analysis provided us as a team important information about what the other guys were doing. “First and for most instill TRUST and Security”

What I Learned: 3. They will know what they want, when they know what they don’t want: I created a Low Fi Prototype based on reasurch and team conversations. After testing the lo fi prototype we decided to go back to the drawing board based on conversations with users. the end result was a superior product.

What I Learned: 4. Walk The Walk: The User Flow was created after a story board was created and User Journey was maped out.

Summary We as a team were able to create a better website then the existing one. Our web site is clean simple to understand creates a sense of lagetamaicy and highlights the good workes this non-profit is doing. My team mates were positive, caring and it was a positive experience for me.
